Roadtrip

RoadTrip:

14 days and 4,500 miles after

Hello! My name is Jesus Camarillo. Welcome to my webpage!

One of my goals is to visit all the states in the United States. What better way to hit up a lot of states in one go than to go on a roadtrip. I will walk you through my experience and places that I visited in my roadtrip starting in San Antonio, TX and ending in Seattle, WA and coming back down. Put on your seatbelt on and enjoy the ride!

    Places I visited:
  1. White Sands, New Mexico
  2. Phoenix, Arizona
  3. Springdale, Utah
  4. Las Vegas, Nevada
  5. Los Angeles, California
  6. San Francisco, California
  7. Seattle, Washington
  8. Wyoming, Michigan
  9. Denver, Colorado

White Sands, New Mexico

New Mexico was our first stop. It took about 10 hours(650 Miles) to get to our first stop driving. It felt like an eternity to get out of Texas. After we got out of Texas, it felt like such a small drive to New Mexico. We visited the White Sands National park. This National park has Great wave-like dunes of gypsum sand have engulfed 275 square miles of desert, creating the world's largest gypsum dunefield

Phoenix, Arizona

Arizona was our next stop. From New Mexico to Arizona, it was 9 hours( 544 miles ). We came to Arizona to visit the famous Grand Canyon National Park. The Grand Canyon has layered bands of red rock encompasses 278 miles of the Colorado River.

Springdale, Utah

Our next stop was in Utah and our destination was Zion National Park. It was only a short 2 hour drive(99 miles). Zion National Park is a southwest Utah nature preserve distinguished by Zion Canyonâ€™s steep red cliffs. We took a 2 hour hike through the river and up the cliffs.

Las Vegas, Nevada

Our next stop was in Las Vegas Nevada. It was a 2 hour and 20 minute drive(152 miles). We went to go see the Jabbawockeez dance Crew perform, we got on the High Roller and we went to the Casinos and lost $20.

Los Angeles, California

Our next stop was in Los Angeles. The drive from Las Vegas to Los Angeles was 4 hours and 20 minutes(271 miles). In Los Angeles, we went to the Santa Monica beach, we visited the Japanese Village and drove around the city to try to find the perfect spot to take a picture with the Hollywood sign.

San Francisco, California

Our next stop was in San Francisco. The drive from Los Angeles to San Francisco was 6 hours and 20 minutes(383 miles). In San Francisco we took a ferry to go to Alcatraz Island. We visited China Town and we went to go see the Golden Gate Bridge. We Also went to the zoo and crossed through the Redwood SkyWalk which is 100 feet off the ground.

Seattle, Washington

Our next stop was in Seattle. From San Francisco it was 14 hours(808 miles). We did take a break in Portland. In Seattle we went to the Space Needle, Chihuly Garden Museam and the Pop Culture Museam. We also went to Pike Place Market and jumped on a boat to go Whale watching.

Wyoming, Michigan

Our next stop was in Wyoming and our destination was Yellow Stone National Park. From Seattle to Yellow Stone it was 12 hours(755 miles). We hired a tour guide to take us around the park. It took an entire day to experience all that this park has to offer. Yellowstone National Park is a nearly 3,500-sq.-mile wilderness recreation area atop a volcanic hot spot. We camped out here to get the full experience of nature and saw all sorts of wild life.

Denver, Colorado

Our final stop before heading back home was Denver.From Wyoming to Denver it was 10 hours and 20 minutes(625 miles). We did 2,500 steps at an incline at the Manitou Incline trail. It took us several hours to get to the top but it was worth the view. Afterwards, we got on the Royal George Route Train for a relaxing ride through a scenic route.

Conclusion

Thank you for joining me in our 14 day and 4,500 mile journey across multiple states. I hope that you enjoyed the ride and that maybe it motivated you to one day go on a roadtrip like I did. It is truly a life changing experience and I know that you won't regret it.
